Rosti Recipe

Description

Rosti originated from Switzerland and it is made with shredded potatoes that are pan fried with butter in a skillet until crispy.

Ingredients

  • 3 to 4 medium starchy potatoes (russets or )
  • 4-5 tbsp butter
  • salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp parsley
  • 1/4 onion, minced
  • 1 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • 1 tsp paprika

Steps

  1. Grate the raw potatoes using a standard box grater (if you prefer mushy potatoes, parboil the potatoes before grating)
  2. Squeeze out the excess liquid then transfer to a bowl (no need to be thorough; 2-3 squeezes suffice
  3. Mix the potato with nutmet, pepper, paprika in the bowl
  4. Mix 3-4 tbsp butter in an 8-inch nonstick or cast-iron skillet and then add in the onion
  5. Add the grated potatoes about 1 inch deep and salt them as desired
  6. Cook the potatoes over medium heat several minutes (about 5 minutes), stirring two or three times with a metal spatula to coat the potatoes evenly with butter
  7. Pat the potatoes into a cake with the spatula and let them cook for 10 minutes
  8. Cover the potatoes and cook for another 7 minutes
  9. When the bottom of the potato cake is golden brown, place a plate that barely fits inside the skillet on top of the potatoes and invert the pan, holding on to the plate
  10. Remove the pan and set it back on the stove; Add the remaining butter to the pan, allowing it to melt
  11. Slide the rosti off the plate and back into the pan, golden side up
  12. Cook for about 15 minutes, pressing down once or twice with the spatula to make the pancake stick together a little more
  13. You can cover the pan to get the potatoes cooked in the middle if desired but remove the cover at least 5 minutes before the end of cooking so that your pancake is crispy on the outside
  14. Slice the rosti up or plate as a whole
